# PL-4 → EXECUTION OS — CONSTITUTIONAL TRANSITION

*v274 · documentation-only · the epilogue that closes the Proposal-Lifecycle era
and opens the Execution OS program. It fixes the historical record, carries the
reserved constitutional seams forward, and establishes the continuity of
principle between what a commitment **is** and what the platform **does** with it.*

## 2. What closes

The **Proposal Lifecycle** (PL-1…PL-4) closes as frozen constitutional
infrastructure. Its subject was the *commitment*: the ledger spine, the
relationship graph, publication and the immutable Offer/Snapshot, and acceptance
and rescission. Its governing principle — **the relation is authoritative; status
is a projection** — is not retired; it becomes the inherited law of everything
built next.

Closed and frozen: invariants I-15…I-30; the publication, acceptance, and
rescission ceremonies; the thread-first lock order; ledger primacy; tenant
isolation; the certification of record. These are not reopened without a genuine
constitutional contradiction.

## 3. What opens

The **Execution OS** opens as net-new construction **above** the frozen commitment
layer. Its subject is what the platform *does* with an approved commitment: the
canonical event, operational release, obligation generation with provenance,
execution evidence, readiness, dependencies, the department domains (culinary,
equipment/warehouse, staffing, transportation, venue), reconciliation, and the
DailyOps orchestration surface. Its constitutional layer is fixed in
`EXECUTION_OS_CONSTITUTIONAL_BOUNDARY.md` as invariants **I-31…I-41**, continuing
the same series and the same standard.

The Execution OS **derives from** the commitment layer and **never writes** it. It
reads `offer_accepted` and `acceptance_rescinded` as authoritative facts; it adds
its own append-only evidence; it projects state, never storing it.

## 4. Reserved seams carried across the transition

These are recognized, recorded, and scheduled — not missing, not blocking:

- **Attested Acceptance** — the PL-4 reserved capability. Slot-provisioned;
  additive; may ship before, during, or after PL-5. Carried forward unchanged.
- **Agreement (PL-5)** — the legal layer. Reserved as an Operational Release
  predicate (boundary Part IV); a business may require it by policy without any
  redesign. Its cardinality over accepted Offers remains the reserved forward
  question.
- **Manual and project work-item classes** — beyond the operational obligation,
  reserved under the I-41 work-item grammar; early slices build only the
  operational-obligation class.

No reserved seam is silently resolved by an early slice.

## 5. Continuity of principle

The Execution OS inherits, without exception, the design laws that carried the
Proposal Lifecycle:

- immutable evidence; append-only ledger; replayable state;
- structural relationships over mutable status; the relation authoritative, status
  a projection;
- thread-first locking; deterministic concurrency; tenant isolation;
- constitutional traceability; additive evolution; default-deny authority;
- immutable customer-visible artifacts;
- the review order — **constitution → SQL invariants → proofs → application → UI**,
  never inverted;
- and the honesty rule the deployed `obligations.ts` already embodies:
  *unknown is not clean* — an unbuilt module reports `computed:false`, never a
  false zero.

The Execution OS does not introduce a new philosophy. It applies the existing one
to a new subject.

vertical slice, built and proven to the PL-4 standard.
